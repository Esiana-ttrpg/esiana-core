Nodes overlap, labels collide, and the tool becomes completely useless for quick reference during a live session.

If we want this intertwined world page to be genuinely useful, we need to design it around **Focus Isolation** and **Chronological Context**.

Instead of showing *everything all at once*, the UI should focus on showing **the world through the eyes of a single entity** at a **specific point in time**.

Here is how we can design the UI and visual interactions to make this highly usable and deeply integrated with your existing timelines.

## 🗺️ The World Hub Layout: "The Split View"

To keep the screen grounded, avoid full-screen abstract maps. Use a **70/30 Split View Canvas**. The left 70% is your clean, interactive visual canvas; the right 30% is a persistent, contextual detail pane that updates automatically based on what you click.

## 🤝 Making Diplomacy Webs Actually Usable

To solve the "spaghetti web" problem, we implement three core UX interactions:

### 1. Dynamic Focus Isolation (Hover & Click)

* **The Default State:** The map displays a faint, low-contrast overview of major factions and geographic regions.
* **The Interaction:** Hovering over or clicking a faction node (e.g., *The Iron Syndicate*) instantly **dims the rest of the web by 80%**.
* **The Visual Result:** The map dynamically lights up only the direct connections to that specific faction. Allies glow emerald green, enemies glow crimson red, and non-aggression pacts glow amber. All crossing lines from irrelevant factions vanish from sight.

### 2. The "Chrono-Scrubber" Timeline Integration

Since your platform already has powerful calendar and timeline mechanics, let's turn them into a temporal navigation tool.

* **The UI:** A interactive timeline slider sits at the bottom of the World canvas view.
* **The Visual Result:** As the DM drags the slider back and forth through campaign history, **the political web dynamically updates in real-time**.
* **The Gameplay Value:** Drag the slider to *Year 400*, and lines show Oakhaven and the Syndicate are at peace. Slide it to *Year 405*, and the connection line snaps, catching fire to symbolize an active war. This instantly handles Legendkeeper's goal of visualizing "cause and effect" over parallel storylines.

### 3. Asymmetric Filter Rails

DMs rarely need to see every corporate feud and religious crusade simultaneously. Provide a lightweight sidebar filter toggle:

* `[x] Show Commercial/Trade Alliances`
* `[ ] Show Secret/Shadow Feuds`
* `[x] Show Military Treaties`

## 🏢 Visualizing Organizational Divisions Cleanly

When switching from the macro political web down into specialized internal organizational views, changing the visual structure based on the organization's **division tab** keeps data incredibly scannable.

### The Cult Interface (Cellular / Blind Layout)

* **The Visual Style:** Decentralized, floating clusters.
* **The Usability Pattern:** The UI doesn't show a clean top-down tree. Instead, it shows isolated "Cells" connected by single, thin, hidden link lines to an obscured or masked "Handler" node. Clicking a cell reveals only what *that specific cell* knows, letting the DM easily track fog-of-war layers during infiltration plots.

### The Alliance Interface (The Flat Council Wheel)

* **The Visual Style:** A circular or ribbon-style layout where all nodes are placed equidistant from the center.
* **The Usability Pattern:** No node sits higher than another. The central hub space inside the circle displays the shared alliance assets (e.g., *The Coalition Treasury, The Combined Fleet Staging Grounds*).

### The Empire Interface (The Feudal Tiered Pyramid)

* **The Visual Style:** Strict, wide, cascading rows.
* **The Usability Pattern:** Highly structured rows explicitly divided by regional boundaries or tiers (e.g., *Imperial Throne -> Sector Governors -> Local Magistrates*). Dragging a Character card into a tier automatically updates their regional metadata tags under the hood.

---

## 👑 The Family Tree "Portrait Strip" Layout

Standard family trees break if someone has four partners, multiple adoptive heirs, and generation-skipping timelines.

* **The UI Fix:** Use an interactive **Horizontal Swimlane** timeline rather than a geometric box tree. Each generation occupies a strict horizontal row pinned to your calendar years.
* **The Visuals:** Clicking any individual portrait highlights their biological parents, active spouses, and direct children with clean neon vectors, while opening their personal chronicle registry directly in the context sidebar pane.
