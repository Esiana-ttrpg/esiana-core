The Recruitment Directory helps you get a feel for how a table plays before you decide to join.

Every listing is a group of real people with their own tone, schedule, playstyle, and comfort level. The goal is not to find the “best” campaign—it is to find a table that feels like a good fit for you.

## Reading a listing

Each listing gives a quick overview of the table:

- **Title and pitch** — A short description of the campaign, setting, or story.
- **Game system** — The rules system the group uses.
- **Genre and themes** — The kinds of stories, moods, or tones the campaign leans toward.
- **Schedule** — When the group usually plays, if listed.
- **Seats** — How many players are currently at the table and whether the group is still recruiting.

Some listings are polished and detailed. Others are brief and conversational. Both are normal.

Opening a listing takes you to the campaign’s lobby page, where you can learn more about the group before requesting a seat.

## What you will find on a lobby page

A campaign lobby may include:

- a longer campaign description
- information about the DM or group
- safety tools and content warnings
- scheduling expectations
- recruitment status
- linked wiki pages under **Before you apply**

Many tables also share documents such as:
- Table Expectations
- House Rules
- Safety Guidelines
- Session Zero notes
- setting or lore introductions

These pages help you understand how that specific group runs their game.

## Finding the right fit

Different tables want very different things from play.

Some groups are:
- highly roleplay-focused
- tactical and combat-heavy
- casual and social
- experimental or rules-light
- long-running and character-driven
- beginner-friendly
- intense, emotional, or horror-focused

None of these approaches are “better” than others. The important thing is finding a group whose expectations match your own.

## What Esiana does not emphasize

Esiana is designed around table fit and shared expectations, not competition between DMs or campaigns.

Public recruitment listings do not include:
- star ratings
- GM rankings
- popularity leaderboards
- public acceptance statistics

Instead, focus on:
- the campaign description
- the group’s tone
- safety information
- scheduling compatibility
- the conversations you have with the table

## Before you apply

Take time to read the lobby carefully before requesting a seat—especially any pages under **Before you apply**.

Those documents often explain:
- table boundaries
- playstyle expectations
- scheduling commitments
- campaign tone
- character creation rules
- onboarding expectations

A little reading up front helps everyone join with clearer expectations and fewer surprises.
