# Getting Started

Esiana helps players find tabletop RPG campaigns that match the kinds of stories, playstyles, and groups they want to join.

You do not need an account to browse public tables. Creating one lets you request seats, track campaigns, and receive updates from tables you join.

## Finding a table

Start in the [Recruitment Directory](/recruitment).

Each listing gives a quick sense of:
- the game system
- themes and tone
- scheduling and availability
- safety tools and content warnings
- who is already at the table

Some campaigns are long-running worlds. Others are short adventures, experimental systems, or casual pickup games. Take time to read the details and look for groups that match the experience you want.

For help understanding listing cards and lobby details, see the [Table Guide](/guides/table-guide).

## Before you join

Opening a campaign lobby gives you a fuller picture of the table:
- the DM’s campaign pitch
- scheduling expectations
- safety notes
- recruitment status
- linked wiki pages under **Before you apply**

Many groups also include onboarding notes, tone guidelines, or house rules before accepting new players.

If a table feels like a good fit, you can send a short join request introducing yourself and what interests you about the campaign.

For more about that process, see [Joining Process](/guides/joining-process).

## Finding the right fit

Not every campaign is meant for every player. A good table fit often matters more than experience level or system mastery.

Consider:
- the campaign’s tone and themes
- how often the group plays
- the balance of roleplay, combat, exploration, or improvisation
- the group’s expectations around safety and comfort tools

You can read more about boundaries, check-ins, and safety practices in [Safety & Comfort](/guides/safety-and-comfort).

## After joining

Once you join a campaign, it appears in your Campaign Hub along with updates, notifications, and quick links back to your active tables.

Over time, your campaigns may develop shared lore, journals, timelines, and session history as the story grows.
