This is how joining an open table works on Esiana—from browsing listings to becoming part of a campaign.

## Steps

1. **Browse** — Use the [Recruitment Directory](/recruitment) to look for tables that match your interests, schedule, and preferred style of play.

2. **Open the lobby** — Each campaign lobby includes the table’s premise, schedule, safety tools, host information, and recruitment details.

3. **Read Before you apply** — Some campaigns include wiki pages such as Table Expectations, Rules, Safety Guidelines, or Session Zero notes. Reading these helps you understand the table before requesting a seat.

4. **Request a seat** — Sign in and send a short introduction about what drew you to the campaign, your experience with the system, or what you hope to find at the table.

5. **Wait for review** — The DM or co-DM reviews requests and may accept, decline, or follow up with additional questions.

6. **Receive a response** — You receive an in-app notification (and email notifications, if enabled) when your request is accepted or declined.

## After you are accepted

Once accepted, you are added to the campaign with the role assigned by the DM (usually player). The campaign will appear in your Campaign Hub, where you can access sessions, wiki pages, timelines, and other shared tools based on the campaign’s permissions.

## If a table is not the right fit

Not every campaign works for every player, and not every player works for every campaign. A declined request is usually about scheduling, group fit, campaign tone, or seat availability—not a judgment of your value as a player.

You are always free to browse other listings and apply to other tables.

## Tips

- Be honest about your schedule, experience level, and interests.
- Focus on mutual fit rather than “impressing” the group.
- If something in a listing is unclear, it is okay to ask questions before applying.
- Read the campaign’s safety notes and expectations carefully before joining.

For more about boundaries, check-ins, and safety practices, see [Safety & Comfort](/guides/safety-and-comfort).
