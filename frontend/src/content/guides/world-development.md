# World Development

World Development is an **optional** living-world layer for your campaign. It generates **narrative suggestions** based on faction trajectories, organization world states, scheduled effects, and campaign time.

**It does not replace Game Master authorship.** You review, edit, approve, or reject every suggestion before it becomes canon (unless you choose a more automated mode).

## How it works

1. You configure **World Development** in **Campaign Settings**.
2. Faction trajectories and world state create **pressure signals**.
3. When time advances (or you click **Suggest developments**), the engine queues suggestions with a **rationale** — why this was proposed.
4. You process the inbox on **Progression › Developments**.

## Modes

| Mode | What happens |
|------|----------------|
| **Off** | No suggestions generated (default). |
| **Manual Review** | Every suggestion waits in your inbox. |
| **Assisted Review** | Minor developments may auto-apply; significant ones wait for you. |
| **Auto Apply** | Eligible suggestions apply within world activity and cooldown limits. |

## World Activity

Choose how much change the campaign generates over a campaign month:

- **Quiet** — typically 1–3 developments
- **Moderate** — typically 6–12 developments
- **Active** — typically 12–20 developments
- **Custom** — set your own range

## Pausing

Use **Pause development** on the Developments page during a focused story arc. This is temporary and operational — not a settings change.

## Readiness

If suggestions are not appearing, check **Development readiness** at the bottom of the Developments page. Common fixes:

- Set era trajectories or world state on active factions
- Configure campaign time
- Unpause development
- Review world activity limits

Configure long-lived behavior in **Campaign Settings › World Development**.
